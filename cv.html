About Me

I am a PhD student in the Department of Ethics, Governance and Society at Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam, supervised by Alessandra Palmigiano and Alexander Kurz. My research intertwines Category Theory, Universal Algebra and Coalgebra, and Modal Logic to explore foundational structures that support reliable reasoning systems.

I focus on non-distributive modal frameworks, causal Kripke semantics, and neighborhood models with an eye toward applications in trustworthy AI. This work involves developing new connections between algebraic, coalgebraic, and interaction-based perspectives on reasoning so that logic remains expressive without sacrificing conceptual clarity.

Through collaborations across LORI, AiML, and ICLA, I aim to build tools that link abstract reasoning principles to practical model design, especially where uncertainty and causality play a central role.
